What We're Working On

The club is just getting started. These are the first four projects we're building out together.

We're building the platform from the ground up: the infrastructure to collect and store data, the systems to test and run strategies, and the research to design them. These four projects cover the full stack of what a quantitative fund actually does.

Engineering / Infrastructure Planning

High-Performance Backtesting Engine

Goal

Build a system that takes a trading strategy and replays it against years of historical market data, tracking portfolio value, trades, and performance metrics along the way. Every strategy the club researches will run through this before anything goes live.

Why it matters

Without a backtesting engine, you have no way to know if a strategy actually works. It is the core research tool for the club. Getting it right, accurate simulation of costs, realistic order fills, and clean performance reporting, is what makes downstream research meaningful.

What it involves
  • Historical market data replay
  • Portfolio and profit/loss tracking
  • Pluggable strategy interface so any member can test their own
  • Transaction cost and slippage modeling
  • Performance metrics: Sharpe ratio, max drawdown, win rate

Engineering / Infrastructure Planning

Trade Execution Engine

Goal

Build the layer that takes a strategy's output, a target position or a signal, and turns it into actual orders that go to a paper trading broker. This is the bridge between a strategy that works in a backtest and one that actually runs in the market.

Why it matters

A backtest is a simulation. Execution is where reality sets in. Prices move, orders fill at different prices than expected, and timing matters. Building this layer is what lets the club actually run strategies and see how they behave in live conditions, not just on historical data.

What it involves
  • Generating orders from strategy signals
  • Broker API integration for paper trading
  • Order management: scheduling, cancellation, retries
  • Fill tracking and position reconciliation
  • Trade logging and monitoring

Data Engineering Planning

Raw Market Data to Clean Research Dataset

Goal

Build the club's internal data backbone: a pipeline that pulls historical and live market data from external APIs, cleans it, and stores it in a format that is easy for anyone to query. Every other project depends on this data being reliable and consistent.

Why it matters

Bad or inconsistent data produces bad research results. If prices are wrong, adjusted incorrectly, or missing dates, every backtest built on top of that data is unreliable. A well-built data pipeline is what makes it possible to trust your research and share it across the club.

What it involves
  • Pulling historical OHLCV data from free and paid APIs
  • Handling corporate actions like splits and dividends
  • Cleaning and normalization pipelines
  • Storing data in research-friendly formats (Parquet, SQL)
  • Internal query tools or a lightweight API for other members

Quantitative Research Planning

Statistical Arbitrage Research Project

Goal

Research and evaluate a pairs trading strategy using statistical arbitrage. The idea is to find two stocks whose prices move together historically, and trade the spread between them when it diverges from its normal range, betting that it will revert.

Why it matters

Pairs trading is one of the oldest and most studied strategies in quantitative finance. It is a good introduction to how quant research actually works: you start with an economic intuition, translate it into a testable hypothesis, build the math around it, and then see if it holds up in data. It is also directly runnable on the backtesting engine once that is built.

What it involves
  • Identifying pairs of related stocks
  • Testing for cointegration using statistical tests
  • Computing the price spread and modeling its behavior
  • Defining entry and exit thresholds based on z-scores
  • Backtesting and evaluating strategy performance
