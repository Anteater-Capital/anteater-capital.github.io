        Why Join?
        
The same systems top quant firms build — built by us.

        
Order management systems, low-latency data pipelines, signal research infrastructure — the architecture we're building mirrors what Two Sigma, Citadel, and Jane Street run in production. We use Rust because microseconds matter and correctness is non-negotiable. If it's sloppy, it doesn't ship.

      

      

        Ownership
        
Real ownership. Genuinely hard problems.

        
Members aren't assigned tasks — they take full ownership of real systems. Your strategy, your code, your results tracked against a shared portfolio. This is production-grade engineering at the systems level: performance matters, the bar is high, and the work will push you further than any class will.

      

    

  


  


  
  

    
Tracks

    

      

        01
        Quantitative Research
      

      

        

          
Statistical Signal Research

          
Find and test quantitative signals in market data. Members develop alpha from scratch — momentum, mean-reversion, cross-sectional factors — and validate them rigorously before anything goes live.

        

        

          
Backtesting & Paper Trading

          
Every strategy runs through a structured backtest and then paper trades against the club's shared portfolio. Results are tracked and reviewed openly week over week.

        

      

    

    

      

        02
        Software Engineering
      

      

        

          
Trading Systems in Rust

          
Build low-latency execution and data infrastructure in Rust. Order routing, position management, and real-time market data handling — engineered for performance and reliability.

        

        

          
Data Engineering

          
Build and maintain the data infrastructure that powers all research — ingesting large datasets of price, volume, and alternative data, normalizing across sources, and storing at scale for fast querying. The foundation every quant fund runs on.

        

      

    

  


  


  
  

    
How it works

    

      

        Step 1
        
Pick a Track

        
Choose Quantitative Research or Software Engineering based on what interests you. No prior experience required — just pick a direction and dive in.

        Start anywhere
      

      

        Step 2
        
Pick a Project

        
Browse open projects within your track or pitch your own. Every member works on something concrete from week one — no busywork, no passive learning.

        Hands-on from day one
      

      

        Step 3
        
Collaborate

        
Work with other members on strategy ideas, architecture decisions, and code reviews. The best work happens at the intersection of quant research and systems engineering.

        Better together
      

      

        Step 4
        
Publish Your Work

        
Write up what you built on the club blog. Document your methodology, results, and code. Strong work gets noticed — by peers, recruiters, and the broader quant community.

        Build your portfolio
      

      

        Step 5
        
Deploy on Your Own

        
Want to trade with real money? Use the club's tested signals and models on your own brokerage account. The risk is yours — so is the upside.

        Real capital, real returns
